The modern VLC — that respects you

What it does

Plays everything

Virtually any codec/container via a proven libmpv/ffmpeg engine, hardware-decoded — HDR/10-bit, 4K/8K, high-fps — with video filters, A–B repeat, speed, snapshots, and resume-from-position.

A modern UI

A glassy, auto-hiding interface that gets out of the way of the picture — the thing VLC never shipped. The video is the hero; the chrome is a clean overlay.

A real media library

Scan your folders into a poster-wall library with opt-in metadata scraping, continue-watching across files, a music library, and search — without standing up a server.

Subtitles done right

SRT/ASS/SSA/PGS/VobSub/WebVTT with full ASS styling, sync, encoding auto-detect, opt-in online fetch, and secondary subtitles (two tracks at once).

Audiophile audio

Gapless, ReplayGain, a 10-band EQ, exclusive-mode output, and bitstream passthrough (AC3/DTS/TrueHD/Atmos) to your receiver — bit-perfect, plus multiple audio tracks.

Streams & casts

Network streams (HLS/DASH/RTSP/SMB/…), YouTube & site URLs via yt-dlp, DVD/Blu-ray/ISO menus, and casting to (or being) a Chromecast/DLNA renderer.

No ads, no spyware

100% local-first. No advertising, no telemetry, no account. The only network actions are explicit and opt-in — and the source is public so you can verify it.

Honest about hard things

Codec patents, encrypted-disc decryption (AACS keys not bundled), and HDR/Dolby Vision platform limits are stated plainly — never faked.
